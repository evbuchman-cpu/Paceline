# Paceline Comprehensive Pricing Strategy

## Executive Summary

Paceline is a personalized ultramarathon race planning software that transforms 30 hours of chaotic DIY planning into a 10-minute, data-driven, anxiety-reducing experience. This strategy document provides everything needed to build a high-converting pricing page and landing page, including tier breakdowns, feature lists, upgrade logic, pricing psychology, competitive positioning, objection handling, brand voice, visual guidelines, and implementation roadmap.

---

## 1. Pricing Objectives & Business Goals

**Revenue Target**: $10,000/month by Month 3; $100,000+/month long-term

**Gross Margin Target**: 84–86%

**Market Position**: Premium, category-defining, but accessible to mid-pack ultrarunners

### Success Metrics
- Conversion rate: 5–10%
- ARPU: $100–120
- Upgrade rates: 30–50% Essential→Custom, 10–20% Custom→Ultra
- Add-on take rates: 40–60%
- Repeat purchase rate: 30–40%
- Refund rate: <3%

---

## 2. Market Research & Competitive Analysis

### Competitors
- **TrainingPeaks** ($19.95/mo): Training, not race execution
- **Strava** (Free–$12/mo): Training/social, not logistics
- **Individual Coaches** ($100–300/mo): Personalized, expensive, not scalable
- **DIY** (Reddit, Excel): Free, time-consuming (30+ hours), error-prone, high anxiety

### Paceline's Differentiator
Only personalized race-day execution planner; 10 minutes vs 30 hours DIY; data-driven, anxiety-reducing, crew logistics included

---

## 3. Pricing Tier Structure

### Tier 1: Paceline Essential – $29 per race

**Features:**
- Basic pacing strategy (simple pacing chart for your race)
- Standard nutrition timeline (generic hydration/calorie guidance)
- Generic crew logistics sheet (one-page cheat sheet)
- Course overview (elevation profile, aid station map)
- Drop bag checklist (what to pack)

**Target**: First-time ultramarathoners, price-conscious runners

**Add-ons** ($10 each): Gear, Mental, Hydration, Nutrition

**Sample Copy:**
> "Your first ultra deserves a solid plan. Get the essentials: pacing, nutrition, crew logistics, and drop bags—all in one simple guide for $29."

---

### Tier 2: Paceline Custom – $99 per race ★ Most Popular

**Features:**
- Everything in Essential
- All Essential add-ons included ($40 value)
- Personalized pace chart (elevation-adjusted, section-by-section, based on your pacing strategy)
- Personalized pacing based on YOUR Strava fitness data
- Crew timing (predicted arrival times at each crew-accessible aid station)
- Cutoff buffer calculator (real-time "am I on track?" math)
- Weather-adjusted strategy (race-week forecast integration)
- Contingency plans (GI issues, blisters, heat/cold protocols)

**Target**: Serious ultrarunners, goal races, returning customers

**Add-ons** ($10 each): Crew Coordination, Race Readiness, Post-Race Journal

**Sample Copy:**
> "Most ultrarunners choose Custom. Get everything in Essential PLUS personalized pacing from your Strava data, crew timing, cutoff buffers, weather adjustments, and built-in contingency plans. Protect your year of training for just $99."

---

### Tier 3: Paceline Ultra Bundle – $497 for 5 races

**Features:**
- 5× Custom Race Guides (everything in Custom tier)
- All Essential & Custom add-ons (×5 races)
- 3 version updates per race = 15 total personalized guide versions
- Bonus: Ultimate Ultramarathon Playbook PDF
- Valid for 12 months from purchase

**Target**: Multi-race runners (2–5 races/year), ambitious goal-setters, coaches

**Value**: $1,145 value for $497 ($33/guide)

**Sample Copy:**
> "Planning multiple races this year? Ultra Bundle gives you 5 Custom guides, ALL add-ons, 15 version updates, priority support, and the Ultimate Ultramarathon Playbook—for just $33 per race. Save $648 vs buying separately."

## 8. Objection Handling

### Objection 1: "Expensive for a PDF"

**Reframe:** "It's not a PDF. It's 30 hours of research done in 10 minutes."

**Proof:** "You've invested $1,200 and 350 hours training. $99 protects that investment."

---

### Objection 2: "I can use Reddit for free"

**Reframe:** "Free advice is generic. This is personalized to YOUR fitness and THIS race."

**Proof:** "How many hours will you spend sorting through conflicting advice?"

---

### Objection 3: "What if the plan doesn't work?"

**Reframe:** "Contingencies built in; money-back guarantee; finish rate data."

**Proof:** "Paceline users finish 15% more often than average."

---

### Objection 4: "Why Ultra if I only do 1–2 races?"

**Reframe:** "Most runners sign up for more; $33/guide even at 2 races saves money."

**Proof:** "70% of first-time 100-mile finishers sign up for another ultra within 6 months."

## 10. Visual Wireframe Suggestions

### Color Palette
- **Trail Canopy green** (#2C5F4D) for headers, CTAs, logo
- **Stone Gray** (#4A5859) for body text, backgrounds
- **Rust Earth** (#C87350) for accent, CTA hover
- **Sandstone** (#D4B896) for subtle backgrounds

### Typography
- **Headers**: Inter Semi-Bold 600
- **Body**: Source Serif Regular 400
- **CTA Buttons**: Inter Semi-Bold 600, 16–18px

### Photography
- Gritty, authentic mid-race moments (mile 60–80)
- Crew interactions, aid station logistics
- Avoid finish line celebrations, podium shots, stock fitness images

### Icons & Graphics
- Elevation line icon (logo)
- Step-by-step icons for "How It Works"
- Feature comparison icons in pricing table

### Layout
- White or light Sandstone background for main sections
- Full-width hero image with overlay for headline/subheadline
- Pricing table centered, Custom tier highlighted
- Testimonials with runner photos, not stock images
- FAQ in collapsible accordion format

## 13. Key Success Factors

✅ **Pricing clarity**: Simple, transparent, no hidden fees

✅ **Social proof**: Testimonials from mid-pack finishers

✅ **Upgrade strategy**: Progressive, not pushy

✅ **Add-on simplicity**: All $10, easy to understand

✅ **Premium positioning**: Coach-level quality at 1/3 to 1/10 coach price

✅ **Monitoring discipline**: Track metrics weekly, iterate monthly, optimize quarterly
